Thinking of getting a dog?



Every year dogs are surrendered, abandoned and lost in our community. Becoming a dog owner is a long term commitment and responsibility.

This website will take you through how you can assist in reducing the statistics and make an informed decision to suit your circumstances.



Know your information

What does it take to be a responsible dog owner?



Know the cost
- according to the RSPCA, the average cost of a dog is over $2000 dollars per year.

Research dog breeds
- the breed of dog can determine the type of home, medical, behaviour and activity levels a dog may require.

Know local Council rules and regulations
- your dog will need to be in a fully fenced yard, be registered and microchipped and desexed.

Know your medical responsibilities
- your dog will need to be desexed, vaccinated, take preventative medicines, visit the vet regularly, be groomed and kept clean in a safe, secure, protected and loving environment.

Dogs are a long term commitment
- dogs can live up to around 15 years. Are your circumstances likely to change? Are you prepared to commit to being responsible for an animal for 15 years?

Think about the dogs in need in your community
- Consider adopting/rescuing a dog from your local shelter instead of purchasing from a breeder, puppy farm or interstate. Just a few are included in the image gallery on this site.

What can you do?

There are several ways you can help


As a community member, awareness of the issue facing our council in regards
to the high number of canines requiring loving, responsible homes is the first step.


The next few slides detail other rewarding ways you can help.


Volunteer

a great way to spend time with animals


Many of our volunteers love animals but are not in a situation to accommodate, afford or take on the long term commitment.


These volunteers are rewarded with plenty of love and appreciation from our animals.


If you are interested in voluteering please contact us

Donate

There are many ways to donate


As our volunteers donate their time, there are other ways to donate to help our animals.


Shelter and care requires money to resource and provide.


Our animals sometimes require urgent or preventative vet care.
Donations of over $2 are tax deductible and very much appreciated.


Donations of food, bedding and preventative medications are also in high demand.


If you would like to donate, please see this information or email us


Adoption and re-homing

A rewarding experience


Join the growing number of members of our community who have adopted and re-homed our needy dogs.


We have many dogs of various ages, breeds and sizes to match with their new owners. Our adoption service
provides consultation, meet and greets and support through the process.
